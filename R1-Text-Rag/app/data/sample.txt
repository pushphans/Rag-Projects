Google is a global technology giant that has fundamentally transformed how humanity accesses and shares information. Founded in 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin while they were Ph.D. students at Stanford University, the company began as an innovative online search engine. Over the decades, it expanded far beyond its original scope, organizing the world's data under its parent company, Alphabet Inc. Today, Google's extensive ecosystem includes universally utilized platforms such as YouTube, Gmail, Google Maps, and the Google Chrome web browser. It also dominates the mobile landscape through Android, the world's most widely adopted operating system. Driven primarily by its highly lucrative advertising infrastructure, Google heavily invests in cutting-edge frontiers like cloud computing and advanced artificial intelligence, most notably through its Gemini models. Headquartered at the Googleplex in Mountain View, California, the company remains a central pillar of modern digital culture, seamlessly integrating its tools into the daily personal and professional lives of billions of people globally.
